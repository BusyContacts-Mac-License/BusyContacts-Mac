## How to Install

1. Download the latest version of BusyContacts for macOS.  
2. Open the DMG installer and move BusyContacts.app to the Applications folder.  
3. Launch the app and allow it through macOS Gatekeeper if required.

## Overview

<a href="#">BusyContacts for macOS</a> reimagines the address book as a relationship dashboard. Instead of just names and phone numbers, it pulls together emails, social feeds and notes into unified contact timelines. This helps you see when you last interacted with someone and what was discussed, without hunting through multiple apps. For people who manage clients, partners or wider communities, that context becomes essential to maintaining trust and continuity.

Contacts can be grouped by projects, companies or roles, and custom fields allow the model to match your world instead of forcing a generic template. Integration with calendars and <a href="#">email histories</a> means that meetings and conversations appear alongside basic details, offering a quick refresher before calls or follow-ups. Tagging and search tools help surface the right person even when you only remember fragments like a topic, location or affiliation.

On macOS, BusyContacts syncs with system accounts while adding its own layer of insights. It is comfortable sitting open beside a calendar and mail client, ready to answer “who is this and what did we talk about last time?” in a glance. Over time, it evolves into a living CRM for individuals and small teams who do not want to move into heavyweight sales platforms just to remember who’s who.
